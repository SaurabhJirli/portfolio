When I first started working with Kafka at Touchcore, I thought it was just "a message queue but fancier." It took a few production issues to understand why it's actually quite different.

## What tripped me up

- **Consumer groups** aren't obvious at first. I kept wondering why only one of my consumers was getting messages — turns out they were in the same group and Kafka was load-balancing across them.
- **Partitions matter** more than I expected. If you want ordering guarantees, you need to think about your partition key early.
- **Offsets are your responsibility** in many setups. Auto-commit sounds convenient until a consumer crashes mid-processing and you lose or double-process messages.

## What helped

- Running Kafka locally with Docker Compose and just watching topics with `kafka-console-consumer` made things click faster than reading docs.
- Writing a small producer/consumer pair outside of work helped me understand the basics without the pressure of production code.
- Reading the Confluent blog posts on exactly-once semantics — even though we don't use exactly-once, understanding the tradeoffs helped.

## The takeaway

Kafka is powerful but has a learning curve that doesn't show up in "hello world" tutorials. The real lessons came from debugging things in production — duplicate events, lag spikes, and rebalancing storms. If you're just starting out, run it locally and break things on purpose.
